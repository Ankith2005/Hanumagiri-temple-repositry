Hanumagiri Temple, Ishwaramangala, Puttur

History

Hanumagiri Temple, also known as Shri Panchamukhi Anjaneya Kshetra, is a revered Hindu temple situated in Ishwaramangala, a village in the Puttur taluk of Dakshina Kannada district, Karnataka, India. The temple is perched atop a hillock, offering a serene and spiritual ambiance to its visitors.

Historical Significance

The temple is believed to have been constructed during the Vijayanagara Empire, approximately 700 years ago. According to local legends, the hill on which the temple stands is part of the Sanjeevini mountain that Lord Hanuman carried to Lanka to revive Lord Rama and his brother Lakshmana. A small portion of the mountain fell at this location, leading to the establishment of the temple, hence the name 'Hanumagiri'.

Architectural Features

The temple is renowned for its 11-foot tall monolithic statue of Panchamukhi Hanuman, carved from black stone. This unique idol features five faces: Hanuman, Narasimha, Varaha, Hayagriva, and Garuda, symbolizing various aspects of divinity and believed to offer protection and blessings to devotees.
